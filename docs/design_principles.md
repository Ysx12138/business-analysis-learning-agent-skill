# Design Principles

## 1. Analysis Is Also Learning

Each analysis task should produce both decisions and learning value.

## 2. Explain Why Before How

Method selection should be justified in business terms before technical detail.

## 3. Translate Data Fields into Business Questions

Field-level understanding is the bridge between raw data and practical decisions.

## 4. Avoid Black-Box Outputs

Outputs should expose assumptions and reasoning, not only final numbers.

## 5. Separate Data Facts from Business Interpretation

The report should explicitly distinguish evidence, interpretation, and assumptions.

## 6. Keep Beginner Users in Mind

Language should be clear, practical, and minimally dependent on prior technical knowledge.

## 7. Prefer Simple Methods Before Complex Methods

Simple and interpretable methods are preferred unless complexity is clearly needed.

## 8. Explain Metrics in Business Language

Metrics should be described using business meaning and decision context, not only formulas.
