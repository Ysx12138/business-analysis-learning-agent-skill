## How to Reuse This Thinking Next Time

When you see a similar dataset, ask:

1. What is the business goal?
2. Which fields represent users, products, time, money, and behavior?
3. Which metric can measure the goal?
4. What comparison or trend should be checked?
5. What does the result mean for business decisions?
6. What assumptions or limitations remain?

## Conclusions NOT to Over-Interpret

- Trend analysis describes the past, not predicts the future.
- Group differences do not prove that the group factor causes the difference.
- High revenue does not mean high profit.
- Small sample sizes produce unstable conclusions.
- Correlation is not causation.
